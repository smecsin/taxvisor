    Eligibilty

    1. LLP shall have at least 2 partners and there is no limit for the maximum numbers of partners.
    2. Every LLP shall have at least 2 Designated Partners and at least one of them shall be a Resident in India
    3. No limit on number of LLP of which a person can become a partner
    4. Any individual or a body corporate can become a partner in LLP
    5. Every LLP requires to have a Registered Office

    What is the benefit to start a LLP?
     No requirement of minimum contribution
     No limit on owners of business
     Lower Registration Cost
     No requirement of compulsory Audit (Audit requires if);
     The contributions of the LLP exceeds Rs. 25 Lakhs, or
     The annual turnover of the LLP exceeds Rs. 40 Lakhs

    What is procedure to start a LLP?
    STEP 1: Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)
    At present scenario for every designated partners must apply for the digital signature for the process of registration of the proposed LLP.

    Step 2: Director Identification Number (DIN)
    You have to apply for the DIN of all the designated partners or those intending to be designated partner of the proposed LLP. The application for allotment of DIN/DPIN has to be made in Form DIR- 3.

    Step 3: Reservation of Name
    1st :- Form 1 is filed for the reservation of name of proposed LLP. But before quoting the name in the form, it is recommended that you use the free name search facility on MCA portal.
    2nd:- The system will provide the list of closely resembling names of existing companies/LLPs based on the search criteria filled up. This will help you in choosing names not similar to already existing names.
    3rd:- You can provide maximum six names in the order of preference in Form 1.
    4th:- The registrar will either approve/reject the name.

    Step 4: Incorporation of LLP
    Form 2 is the application form for the incorporation of the LLP.

    Details to be filled in the form:-
    • All the details in the form must be filled correctly like – total number of partners and designated partners, amount of partner’s contribution, etc.
    • You have to pay the prescribed registration fee based on the contribution of partners in the proposed LLP.
    • The form must be digitally signed by a person named in the incorporation document as a designated partner having DIN. Also, it has to be digitally signed by an advocate/Company Secretary/Chartered Accountant/Cost Accountant in practice.
    • On the submission of the form, if the registrar is satisfied, they will register the proposed LLP.
    TIME :- It takes 15-20 days for the registration of LLP subject to government processing time and submission of necessary documents.

    Step 5: File Limited Liability Partnership Agreement
    LLP agreement governs the mutual rights and duties amongst the partners and also between the LLP and its partners.
    Form 3 for LLP agreement has to be filed within 30 days of the date of incorporation.
    The LLP Agreement has to be printed on Stamp Paper. The value of Stamp Paper is different for every state.

    Whether LLP Agreement would be mandatory for all LLPs and hpw much stamp duty is required to be paid on the LLP Agreement?
    Ans. As per provisions of the LLP Act, It is mandatory to prepare and file the LLP agreement with Rregistrar of Companies.

    The Stamp Duty payable for LLP Agreement is subject to which state the LLP is incorporating and the Stamp duty is applicable to the State Stamp Act of that State.

    What is the manner in which a partner of an LLP can bring his contribution? How will it be recorded/disclosed in the accounts?
    Partner’s contribution may consist of both tangible and/or intangible property and any other benefit to the LLP. The monetary value of contribution of each partner shall be accounted for and disclosed in the accounts of the limited liability partnership in the manner as may be prescribed in the rules (see section 32).

    What is the yearly compliance to a LLP?
     Annual Return: - Form 11 (Within 60 Days of Financial Year Closing i.e. 30th May)
     Accounts and Insolvency: - Form 8 (Within 30 Days from Half Financial year closing i.e. 30th October)

    Whether audit of all LLPs would be mandatory?
     Audit of LLPs shall be mandatory.
     However small LLPs has being exempted from the requirement of audit through notification by the Central Government.

    Whether Minute book would be maintained? What are the compliances relating to Meetings of LLP?
    Minute book should be maintained to record minutes of meetings of partners.
    There is no provision for regular meeting of members of LLPs. Partners can decided when and how to meet or as may be laid down in LLP Agreement.

    Can I convert a Private or public Company in to LLP and vice versa?
    Yes, by filing of specified forms with the registrar of Companies the private company or an unlisted public company can get converted into limited liability partnership along with prescribed fees and ROC will issue a Certificate of Registration under his seal within the prescribed time.
